Malabar Chicken Curry

Description

Malabar is a region in Kerala and is dominated by the Muslim population. This region has a unique way of cooking from the rest of the state, and the slight difference in the cooking method is evident in their cuisine.

Malabar Chicken Curry is a Kerala-style (specifically Malabar region) chicken curry cooked in coconut oil and has a distinct taste from the use of curry leaves, spices, coconut paste, and coconut oil.

The sauce is luscious and creamy, and the coconut flavor shines through. It is pretty easy to bring together as well.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Oil
  • Curry Leaves
  • Spice Powders
  • Tomatoes
  • coconut Paste

How to make Malabar Chicken Curry

  1. Heat coconut oil in a medium-sized pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, add onions and fry until they turn slightly brown (8-10 minutes). Stir frequently while frying.
  3. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste and fry for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, Kashmiri red chili powder, and black pepper powder and fry for 10-15 seconds.
  5. Now add tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes. Keep mashing the tomatoes from the back of a ladle.
  6. Add chicken and salt and cook for 3-4 minutes on high heat.
  7. Add fresh coconut paste and Â½ cup of water to the pan. Mix well.
  8. Reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan using a tight-fitting lid and cook until the chicken is tender. It will take 30-35 minutes.
  9. Stir a few times while cooking.
  10. Open the lid and check for salt. Add more salt if needed and cook for another minute.
  11. For tempering, heat coconut oil in a small pan over medium-high heat.
  12. Once the oil is hot, add mustard seeds and let them crackle for 2-3 seconds. Add coconut and shallots.
  13. Fry until they turn slightly brown, stirring frequently.
  14. Pour the tempering over the chicken curry and mix well. Serve hot with pathiri or coconut rice.
